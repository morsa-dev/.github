<div align="center">
  <h1>Morsa</h1>
  <p><strong>Governance layer for AI coding</strong></p>
  <p>Control how AI is used across your engineering organization.</p>
</div>

<br>

<table>
  <tr>
    <td valign="top" width="50%">
      <h2>Why</h2>
      <ul>
        <li>No centralized way to manage standards across projects</li>
        <li>Rules drift and become inconsistent over time</li>
        <li>No guarantee that guidelines are actually applied</li>
        <li>Difficult to roll out improvements across all codebases</li>
        <li>Limited visibility for engineering leadership</li>
      </ul>
    </td>
    <td valign="top" width="50%">
      <h2>What Morsa provides</h2>
      <ul>
        <li>Organization-level standards management</li>
        <li>Consistent application across teams, tools, and projects</li>
        <li>Global rollout without modifying every repository</li>
        <li>Enforcement, not just documentation</li>
        <li>Visibility and control for engineering leadership</li>
      </ul>
    </td>
  </tr>
</table>

<br>

<h2>What this enables</h2>
<ul>
  <li>Reliable and controlled AI-assisted development</li>
  <li>Faster and safer AI adoption across teams</li>
  <li>Consistent code quality and security standards</li>
  <li>Organization-wide rollout of improvements</li>
  <li>Reduced operational and engineering risk</li>
</ul>

<br>

<h2>How it works</h2>
<ol>
  <li>Define standards, rules, and context centrally</li>
  <li>Attach them to teams, projects, or environments</li>
  <li>Integrate with AI tools and development workflows</li>
  <li>Enforce and track usage across your organization</li>
</ol>

<br>

<h2>Scope</h2>
<p>Morsa is not a prompt tool.</p>
<p>It is an infrastructure layer for governance, standardization, visibility, and control across AI-driven development.</p>
